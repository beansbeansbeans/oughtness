About

What is this place?

Oughtness is a tool for prioritizing causes through crowdsourcing. The site is inspired by the GifGif and Place Pulse projects.

Users compare two causes at a time in terms of their criticalness or tractability. The data page shows the causes ranked by priority based on all the votes collected on the site.

What's the point?

We hope our data can contribute to research on cause prioritization - a field concerned with the question of where humanity should apply its resources in order to achieve the greatest positive impact. Traditionally, researchers generate independent estimates of cause priority in order to answer this question. By contrast, we ask users to make head-to-head comparisons between causes. There is evidence that the latter approach to determining priority may have certain advantages.

Methodology

Our methods are heavily influenced by GiveWell, a charity research organization. The twelve causes presented on the site are from their list of policy areas most worthy of greater investment, and criticalness and tractability are two criteria from their framework for evaluating causes.

In order to derive overall rankings for causes from pairwise comparisons, we made use of the analytic hierarchy process (AHP). The AHP is a widely used technique for making complex decisions involving multiple alternatives and criteria. For more details, check out the methodology section of our data page.
